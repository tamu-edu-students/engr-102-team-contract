## Team Contract
Working in groups enhances activities used in active and collaborative learning and for practice. Team skills are valued by industry; real engineers frequently work in teams. In ENGR 102 you are expected to
- Work cooperatively with your team, not compete against them
- Learn interdependently with your team and your peers
- Learn to be accountable to your team and have your team be accountable to you
- Work with your team to complete team assignments in class
- Meet with your team outside of classes to complete assignments when needed
- Rely on and trust your peers, as well as the faculty and PTs to learn the course material

A team contract is a document that outlines an agreement between all team members about how your team will operate. Your contract doesn’t have to be long or full of legalese, but it should address a number of items related to working together.

Create a shared Google document named `team_XX_contract` where `XX` is your team number. As a team, discuss the items listed below. Write a summary of your responses to each bullet point below in your document. Write your responses in complete sentences unless otherwise noted. There is no maximum word limit, but it is likely that it will take a minimum of 50 words to thoroughly explain each item. Please include the names of all team members and your section number at the top of the document.
- Preferred contact information for all team members. Can be email, phone number, GroupMe, Discord, etc. (a list is okay)
- ENGR 102 goals for the semester (a list is okay)
- When and where should team meetings be held outside of class?
- How will you decide to split the work among team members?
- What do you consider to be the minimum criteria to receive credit on a team assignment? Examples may include (but are not limited to): attendance, brainstorming ideas, writing code, debugging, etc. 
- How do you plan to resolve conflicts? Consider these scenarios:
  - What should happen if a team member does not follow through on a commitment?
  - How will you handle different work habits of individual team members?
  - What should happen if a team member decides that a low grade is acceptable?
  - You may find it helpful to read the file Coping with Hitchhikers and Couch Potatoes on Teams.pdf
- The Aggie Code of Honor: "An Aggie does not lie, cheat or steal or tolerate those who do."

Each team member should sign (type) their name ***from their own account*** at the bottom of the document to indicate they agree with the contents of your team contract. The activity log will be checked to ensure each team member contributed. Any future team issues that cannot be resolved by your team using your team contract will be decided by your instructor.


## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **Do I really have to fill out a team contract?** YES! This is a good way to set up some guidelines on how y'all plan to work together. Plus, if you run into team issues later on, the teaching team can help you resolve them better.

2. **Yeah, but why do I have to fill one out? Can't we just talk about it for a few minutes?** Benjamin Franklin once said "If you fail to plan, you are planning to fail." This contract will help your team work together better, but more importantly will help your team resolve conflicts that may arise in the future. Any issues that come up that your team is not able to resolve on their own will be decided by your instructor.
